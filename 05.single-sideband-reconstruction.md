:::::{tab-set}
::::{tab-item} Ideal acquisition
- In the absence of aberrations, $\chi(\bm{k}) = 0$ &rarr; two redundant overlap regions
:::{math}
:label: eq_gamma_ideal
\Gamma(\bm{q,k}) = A(\bm{k}) A(\bm{k-q}) - A(\bm{k}) A(\bm{k+q})
:::
- Simple inversion of @eq_forward_model, known as SSB ptychography [https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ultramic.2014.09.013]
:::{math}
:label: eq_ssb_estimator
\phi_{\mathrm{SSB}}(\bm{q}) = \sum_{\bm{k}} A(\bm{k}) A(\bm{k-q}) G(\bm{q,k})
:::  
::::

::::{tab-item} Aberrated acquisition
- In the presence of aberrations &rarr; probe overlaps acquire phase structure
:::{math}
:label: eq_gamma_aberrated
\Gamma(\bm{q,k}) \propto \mathrm{exp}[\mathrm{i} (\chi(\bm{k})-\chi(\bm{k-q}))] - \mathrm{exp}[-\mathrm{i} (\chi(\bm{k+q})-\chi(\bm{k}))]
:::
- Equation @eq_forward_model can be inverted using a phase-compensated kernel [@https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ultramic.2016.09.002]
:::{math}
:label: eq_ssb_phase_compensated_estimator
\phi_{\mathrm{SSB}'}(\bm{q}) = \sum_{\bm{k}} \frac{\Gamma^*(\bm{q,k})}{|\Gamma(\bm{q,k})|} G(\bm{q,k})
:::
